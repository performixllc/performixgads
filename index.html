SaaS · B2B Lead Gen

Conversion tracking was counting email opens as purchases

A SaaS company reported a 12% "conversion rate" — their CEO was thrilled. Their actual purchase conversion rate was 0.8%. Smart Bidding was optimizing against ghost data.

9 of 11 Conversion actions set to Primary
−62% CPA drop after tracking fix
$0.01 Assigned value for "new customer"
1,500% ROAS target — set way too high
Key Finding

All micro-conversions (page views, video plays, chatbot opens) were set as Primary goals. Smart Bidding was optimizing for engagement, not revenue — and had been for 8 months.

HVAC · Wholesale Distribution

Top competitor paused their PMax — $10M in inventory went dark

Our client's #1 competitor had historically dominated Google Shopping with 75M+ lifetime impressions. Then they went dark. We spotted it first and helped our client move in.

−250K Competitor impression drop (one month)
+178% Client Shopping impressions gained
$1.2M Competitor's historical PMax spend
942% Competitor's reported ROAS (inflated)
Key Finding

The competitor's ROAS was inflated by GA4-imported goals with no click-based attribution. Their brand campaign was capped at $100/day despite a 12% CVR. A textbook example of a self-sabotaged account.
